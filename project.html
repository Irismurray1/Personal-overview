Background Information

My name is Iris Murray and I am 21 years old. I orginally come from Dunfermline, Fife; but I moved across to Edinburgh in 2021 in the hope of furthering my education. I am currently in my first year of studying Sound Design at Edinburgh Napier University


I decided to pursue a degree in sound design as music and sound in general has always been something i've found facsinating. I had intially decided to study music, however I ended up dropping out in first year when i realised the course was very different from what i had expected. I reapplied twice to various music courses but ultimately decided that they weren't for me & that I was infact not willing to relocate as I had built a stable life for myself in Edinburgh. I did find this challenging as the majority of people around me were studying, had graduated, or had some sort of plan. This left me feeling like most things I did were infact a means to an end.
I have always believed that everything happens for a reason, and there must be some explaination as to why I inevitably found an excuse not to pursue studying music despite putting so much effort into course applications and portfolios. I realised that every year I had applied to study music I had also applied to study Sound Design at ENU, maybe there was a reason that although it wasnt my first choice, it was still consistently in my choices. I looked into it properly and decided I might as well give it a go, as it really piqued my interest. To be completely honest I am so glad that I took the leap in pursuing this course, as so far i have found all aspects of it very interesting.
This even stretches to the more digital aspects of the course, which is surprising as I have always found technology extremely boring and accepted that its something that I just dont 'get'. This is of course mortifying as being part of the 'Digital Native' generation I feel as though I am expected to breeze through understanding new & current technology. I would go as far as to brand myself a technophobe, see article here for more information.
Overall, I feel extremely positive about the course so far and am excited for what it wil bring in the future, however i might be viewing it through the rose-tinted glasses of delusion as I am only in my first trimester of my first year.

Interests in chosen degree

Before beginning this course, my intial understanding of Sound Design was very surface level. As a musician I was familiar with music production and how music is used in other settings, such as retail and advertising. In addition to this I was moderately familiar with sound in films; and how music, foley, and other sound effects help convery emotion or storylines. Beyond this, I was unsure of the large scope of elements sound design actually covers.
As I am becoming more familiar with how & where sound is used, I have realised that it is something that has always been a huge part of my life, even stretching back to when i was very young, to before I understood what the concept of sound even was.
For example, from a young age i have listened to audiobooks to help me sleep, I specifically enjoyed audiobooks containing sound effects and music as opposed to audiobooks consisting solely of narration. Specifically "Horrible Histories" audiobooks based on the book series of the same name by Author Terry Deary Deary. During one of the modules within the course, we learned about and as a group created soundscapes containing narration. This was intially an intimdating project, however upon learning what this actually consisted of, I realised it was something i was familiar with. It clicked that the Horrible Histories audiobooks I had religiously listened to for years were in fact soundscapes and I was infact familiar with the concept.
